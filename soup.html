Sundubu-jjigae Recipe

Sundubu-jjigae is a popular Korean stew that is known for its spicy and savory flavors. It is made with soft tofu, vegetables, and sometimes seafood, all cooked in a spicy broth made with gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes) and other seasonings. The dish is typically served hot with a bowl of rice and various banchan (Korean side dishes). Sundubu-jjigae is a comforting and satisfying meal that has become a staple in Korean cuisine and is loved by many around the world.

Recipe

Ingredients:
  1. 1 package of soft tofu (sundubu)
  2. 1/2 cup of ground pork or beef
  3. 1/2 onion, sliced
  4. 1/2 zucchini, sliced
  5. 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  6. 2 green onions, chopped
  7. 1/2 tablespoon of gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
  8. 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  9. 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  10. 2 cups of anchovy or beef broth
  11. Salt and pepper to taste
Instructions:
  1. Heat the sesame oil in a pot over medium heat and add the garlic, onion, zucchini, and ground pork/beef. Stir-fry for a few minutes until the meat is browned.
  2. Add the gochugaru and continue to stir-fry for another minute.
  3. Add the broth and soy sauce to the pot and bring it to a boil.
  4. Reduce the heat to medium-low and add the soft tofu, gently breaking it up into bite-sized pieces with a spoon.
  5. Simmer for 5-10 minutes until the tofu is cooked through and the flavors have melded together.
  6. Add salt and pepper to taste, and sprinkle the chopped green onions on top.
